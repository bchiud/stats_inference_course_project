1 Intro

This project investigates the exponential distribution in R and compare it with the Central Limit Theorem. We use the rexp(n, lambda) to generate our distributions, and observe 1000 simulations of 40 samples each with a lambda = 0.2.

We aim to show the following:

  1. Show the sample mean and compare it to the theoretical mean of the distribution
  2. Show how variable the sample is (via variance) and compare it to the theoretical variance of the distribution
  3. Show that the distribution is approximately normal

5 Distribution

  • We show if our distribution is normal

6 Conclusion

Our analysis of 100 simulations of 40 samples each shows that the mean, variance and distrubtion of our sample is roughly equal to that of the population mean, variance, and distribution. This is consistent with the characteristic of the Central Limit Theorem.

simulator<-function(n,lambda,simulation_count,seed=1337){
  set.seed(seed) # make our results repeatable
  simulation_means<-data.frame(mean=numeric(simulation_count)) # empty data frame to store our 1000 simulations
  for(i in 1:simulation_count){
    simulation_means[i,]<-mean(rexp(n,lambda)) # get mean for our 1000 simulations of 40 samples each
  }
  simulation_means
}
exponentials<-40
lambda<-0.2
simulation_count<-1000
simulation_result<-simulator(exponentials,lambda,simulation_count)
head(simulation_result,10)
theoretical_mean<-1/lambda
sample_mean<-mean(simulation_result$mean)
theoretical_variance<-(1/lambda)^2/exponentials

ggplot(
